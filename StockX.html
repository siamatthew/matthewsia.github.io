Cash for kicks

Sneakers have always been a passion of mine. For this project, I decided to research on the business of reselling sneakers for profit. I analyzed the potential sneakers resale value based on the existing consumer datasets from StockX (sneaker resale online marketplace) using Power Query and presented the insights with a visual and interactive dashboard using Microsoft Excel.

Backstory

My very first pair of sneakers were the Nike Dunk High, which I paid S$80 (US$60) for in 2004. They are currently valued at US$450 on StockX. That’s a return capital of more than 750% in seventeen years or so.

Who would have thought that the sneaker reselling market would one day become mainstream? E-commerce sites such as StockX and Goat have made it accessible for people who once did not have access to these exclusive releases.

Discovering the most valuable sneakers

For this project, I explored a StockX dataset which contains the sales information for every transaction made in the US of Off-White and Yeezy brands from 2017 to 2019.

From their data set, there are 8 variables to work with:
• Order date
• Brand
• Sneaker name
• Sale price
• Retail price
• Release date
• Shoe size
• Buyer state

With that, I added another variable to the data set – the return of investment (ROI). I then created a visualisation which looked at the sales trend, top 10 sneakers with the best (ROI) and the region where these sneakers are the most “flippable”. Interactive features were added to the dashboard to provide further insights.

Based on preference, the data can be filtered to view the most in-demand sneakers and their ROI. A specific sneaker model can also be analyzed in detail – from the number of pairs being sold over different years to the ROI of that pair.

When the respective year is selected, the dashboard will show the sales and profit based on the monthly performance of the year.

The best investment is Air Jordan 1 - Retro High Off White Chicago, with a retail price of US$190. Its ROI increases every year, with 2017 at 657%, 2018 at 957% and 2019 at 1256%.

Here’s a table summary of the Air Jordan 1-Retro: In 2019, this shoe has a profit of $2,386. Ka-ching!

When people flip sneakers, what they are really doing is buying exclusive models at retail price, waiting until the supply of the shoes decreases, and then reselling them at a much higher price.

If you want to focus on taking smaller gains in short term trends, Yeezy would be a good pair to invest because it is always in demand according to the StockX dataset. Recently, a pair of Yeezy (worn by Kayne West no less) sold for a record 1.8 million dollars!

The above visual illustrates where most of the buyers are coming from – the highest amount of sales are in California and New York. Even though these two major cities had the highest sales overall, it is also interesting to note that other regions also have substantial amount of sales – StockX has made these brands more accessible to areas where it was previously lesser known.

In conclusion, selling sneakers is a lucrative business and can potentially lead to a windfall of profits in a short amount of time.
